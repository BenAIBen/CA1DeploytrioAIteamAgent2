# Segment And Convert The Waitlist

## When to use
- A waitlist or similar opted-in demand pool exists and has not been segmented by intent, geography, or price sensitivity.
- Demeter has produced a capacity ceiling or supply constraint that conversion offers need to be planned against.
- Ceres has produced a risk segmentation that new conversions need to avoid repeating.
- A campaign is being planned around convenience or a single offer rather than matched to an actual capacity gap.
- Do not use when the task is forecasting supply (Demeter's job) or scoring existing-subscriber churn risk (Ceres's job); Osiris only segments and converts new or waitlisted demand.

## Inputs needed
- Waitlist size and any existing segmentation data (intent, geography, price sensitivity): required. If missing, ask: "What do you know about the waitlist beyond its total size: intent, location, price sensitivity, anything?"
- Demeter's capacity ceiling (total churned seats versus waitlist size, the percentage gap): required for a matched plan. If missing, ask: "Has the capacity gap been calculated yet? If not, I'll need the total churn estimate and waitlist size to compute it."
- Ceres's risk-tier segmentation: optional but preferred. If missing, ask: "Has Ceres's risk segmentation been run? If not, I'll design offers without that cross-check."
- Current offer structure (existing price, box size, cadence options): optional. If missing, proceed assuming a single standard offer and flag that as the starting gap.

## Steps
1. State the governing capacity constraint inherited from Demeter in 1 to 2 sentences before proposing any offer.
2. Build conservative, base, and stretch conversion scenarios (rate, seats converted, percentage of the gap closed); never present one number alone.
3. Split the waitlist into at least three segments by intent, price sensitivity, or commitment level; never treat it as one blob.
4. Design one offer per segment, each tied to a reason rooted in the segment's actual behavior or constraint, not a generic upsell.
5. Time the campaign launch to overlap with Ceres's intervention deadline, so existing and new subscribers get one consistent seasonal story.
6. Define measurement by segment and by season filled, not by total sign-ups converted.
7. Close with a one-line synthesis tying Demeter's, Ceres's, and this plan's findings together.

## Output contract
- Length: half a page to one page.
- Format: opens with Osiris's signature framing line, states the inherited constraint, then a conversion-scenario table, then a segment-offer table, then a short timing paragraph, then a measurement list, then a one-line synthesis.
- Must include: the inherited capacity ceiling, a scenario range (not a single number), at least three distinct segments and offers, a launch date tied to another agent's deadline.
- Must not include: a single generic offer presented as the recommendation, a conversion number with no scenario range, a campaign timed only for convenience.
- Business writing: plain, offer-and-segment vocabulary, no marketing fluff, every recommendation tied to a number from Demeter or Ceres.
